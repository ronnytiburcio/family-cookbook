Arroz con Pollo

R
Added by Abuela Rosa · March 2024

"This is the dish that brought your abuelo to my door every Sunday. The secret is patience — let the rice get that crispy bottom, the socarrat. That's where all the love lives."

Ingredients

  • ● 4 lbs chicken thighs, bone-in
  • ● 3 cups long grain rice
  • ● 1 cup sofrito (see page 3)
  • ● 1 can tomato sauce
  • ● 4 cups chicken broth
  • ● 1 tbsp sazón completa
  • ● ½ cup olives with pimientos
  • ● 1 packet sazón con azafrán
  • ● Fresh cilantro, salt & pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. 1
    Season the chicken generously with sazón completa, salt, and pepper. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes — overnight in the fridge is even better.
  2. 2
    In a large caldero or dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the chicken on all sides until deeply golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  3. 3
    In the same pot, add the sofrito and cook until fragrant, about 3 minutes. Add the tomato sauce and sazón con azafrán. Stir well.
  4. 4
    Add the rice and stir to coat every grain. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Nestle the chicken pieces into the rice, add olives.
  5. 5
    Cover tightly and reduce to low heat. Cook for 30–35 minutes. Do NOT lift the lid — trust the process, mija.
  6. 6
    Uncover, fluff gently with a fork, and let the socarrat form on the bottom for 5 more minutes. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
Abuela's Note ✍
Don't rush the socarrat. Your abuelo used to say you could hear when the rice was ready — it crackles and sings. That sound means dinner is almost on the table.
